# START ANY PRESENTATION RIGHT

## When to Use

Use this prompt as the **opening component** of any presentation (interview case study, pitch, conference talk, product review). Apply it in **Phase 1 (Foundation)** of the presentation-mastery workflow, after you've established your key message and narrative arc but before designing slides.

This prompt takes 10-15 minutes and delivers the single most valuable opening you can craft.

## Why It Works

Patrick Winston's empowerment promise forces you to define the *single most valuable insight* your audience will gain. Unlike weak openers ("Thank you for having me"), the empowerment promise creates immediate tension: *the audience wants to know what they'll learn.* It's the difference between optional and essential.

The first 60 seconds are not introduction—they're justification for the next 60 minutes.

## Trade-Offs

**What it does well:**
- Creates urgent, compelling reason to listen
- Forces you to crystallize the core value of your presentation
- Eliminates filler and weak opens
- Takes 10 minutes to craft and pays dividends in attention
- Gives you the exact words to open with (not improvised)

**What it sacrifices:**
- Requires honesty about what your presentation actually delivers
- Can't hedge or include multiple competing promises
- Demands you cut anything that doesn't serve the opening premise

## Related Techniques

- **Specificity Principle:** "You'll be able to do X" beats "You'll learn about Y"
- **Audience-Centric Framing:** Promise is for them, not about you
- **Tension & Resolution:** Promise creates tension; rest of presentation resolves it
- **Sunk Cost Attention:** Audience invested in knowing the answer to your opening promise

## Example Use Case

**Scenario:** You're presenting an autonomous delivery strategy to Instacart leadership.

**Weak open:** "Today I'm going to talk about autonomous delivery."

**Strong empowerment promise:** "By the end of this presentation, you'll know exactly which customer segments benefit most from autonomous delivery, the economics that make it profitable, and a 90-day pilot that proves it works before we scale."

That's specific. That's outcome-driven. Audience knows why they should listen.

## How to Use This Prompt

1. Gather: your presentation topic, target audience, what you want them to know/do
2. Run prompt with these inputs
3. Save the empowerment promise (you'll reference it constantly)
4. Use the "First 60 Seconds" script verbatim when you present
5. Use "What to Cut" as a filter for opening content

## Common Pitfalls

- **Too vague:** "You'll understand autonomous delivery" → Too broad, not specific enough
- **Too presumptuous:** "You'll learn why my solution is the only way" → Overconfident, audiences reject
- **Too long:** Opening promise > 2 sentences → Loses impact
- **No tension:** "I'll walk you through three points" → No reason to stay
