# Cut Your Presentation Down Prompt

## When to Use

This prompt is indispensable for refining and optimizing your presentation content for maximum impact and brevity. Use it when you need to:
- Ruthlessly cut down overly verbose or text-heavy slides.
- Ensure that every slide communicates only one clear idea.
- Sharpen bullet points into concise, headline-style language.
- Make your presentation more engaging and easier for the audience to digest.
- Transform a draft into a polished, professional, and efficient deck.

## Why It Works

This prompt acts as a "ruthless" editor, applying strict editorial principles to presentation content. It combats the common problem of overcrowded slides by enforcing rules like "If a slide has more than one idea, split it into two" and "No bullet point longer than one line." This systematic approach to cutting noise and sharpening the message ensures that every element on the slide serves a purpose, making the presentation much more effective and audience-friendly.

## Architecture

This prompt uses a structured format to guide the AI's editing and content refinement process:

1.  **Role:** Defines the AI's persona as a slide editor who ruthlessly cuts, sharpens, and simplifies.
2.  **Task:** Clearly states the goal: reviewing and rewriting existing content for tightness, clarity, and slide-friendliness.
3.  **Steps:** Outlines a logical sequence for identifying issues, rewriting, sharpening bullet points, and ensuring single-idea slides.
4.  **Rules:** Imposes strict quality constraints, such as splitting multi-idea slides, limiting bullet point length, cutting irrelevant content, and demanding a sharper rewrite.
5.  **Output:** Specifies the desired structure for the editing results, including flagged slides, rewritten content, and a final slide count.

## Trade-Offs

**What it does well:**
- Creates highly concise, impactful, and audience-friendly presentation slides.
- Eliminates clutter and ensures every slide delivers a single, clear message.
- Dramatically improves audience engagement and comprehension.
- Helps the presenter focus on key points during delivery.

**What it sacrifices:**
- Requires the user to provide their current presentation content.
- The AI's editing is based on its interpretation of "noise" and "signal," which might require some human review to ensure no critical nuances are lost (though the prompt aims to mitigate this).
- May significantly alter the original phrasing, necessitating a review to ensure the presenter's voice is maintained.

## Related Techniques

-   **Persona Priming:** Assigns an expert editor role to guide the AI's critical content analysis.
-   **Constraint-Based Prompting:** Uses strict rules (e.g., max bullet length, one idea per slide) to enforce editorial standards.
-   **Content Refinement:** Guides the AI to improve clarity, conciseness, and impact of existing text.
-   **Editing & Rewriting:** Directly leverages the AI for advanced text manipulation and optimization.

## Example Use Case

You've finished writing all the content for your presentation, but you notice some slides are too dense, and bullet points are too long. You'd use this prompt to have the AI ruthlessly edit your entire deck: for example, taking a slide with three paragraphs of text and rewriting it into three sharp, single-line bullet points, or identifying a slide that discusses both a problem and a solution, and suggesting it be split into two distinct slides.
