# Design Every Slide Before You Write Prompt

## When to Use

This prompt is ideal for structuring a presentation after the overall blueprint is established. Use it when you need to:
- Translate your presentation blueprint into a detailed slide-by-slide structure.
- Ensure a logical and natural flow between all slides.
- Define a clear title and purpose for each individual slide.
- Proactively identify and eliminate unnecessary or redundant slides.

## Why It Works

This prompt acts as a presentation architect, focusing on the structural integrity and flow of the entire deck. By demanding a title and purpose for every slide, it forces a disciplined approach to content organization. The rules reinforce best practices for slide design, such as "Every slide must have one job" and "No filler slides," leading to more impactful and cohesive presentations. It prevents the common mistake of building slides in isolation or writing content without a clear structural plan.

## Architecture

This prompt uses a structured format to guide the AI's presentation structuring process:

1.  **Role:** Defines the AI's persona as a presentation architect, implying expertise in structural design and flow.
2.  **Task:** Clearly states the goal: designing a complete slide-by-slide structure.
3.  **Steps:** Outlines a logical sequence for assigning titles, defining purposes, ensuring transitions, and identifying potential merges or cuts.
4.  **Rules:** Imposes strict quality constraints, such as ensuring each slide has a single job and that transitions are smooth and logical.
5.  **Output:** Specifies the desired structure for the slide outline, making it easy to review and build upon.

## Trade-Offs

**What it does well:**
- Ensures a cohesive and logical flow throughout the presentation.
- Forces clarity and focus for each individual slide.
- Helps in preventing bloated decks by identifying unnecessary slides upfront.
- Accelerates content creation by providing a clear framework to write into.

**What it sacrifices:**
- Requires initial input of topic and total slide count.
- The quality of the slide structure is dependent on the clarity of the initial presentation blueprint (if one was created).

## Related Techniques

-   **Persona Priming:** Assigns an expert role to guide the AI's structural design process.
-   **Structural Decomposition:** Breaks down the presentation into individual, purposeful slides.
-   **Constraint-Based Prompting:** Uses rules to enforce best practices for flow and content discipline.
-   **Outline Generation:** Guides the AI to produce a detailed, actionable outline for the presentation.

## Example Use Case

After creating a blueprint for a sales pitch, you'd use this prompt to design the exact sequence of slides. The AI would provide a title and purpose for each slide (e.g., "Slide 1: Title - The Current Market Landscape (Purpose: Set context for the problem)," "Slide 2: Title - The Customer Pain Point (Purpose: Establish urgency)"), ensuring a compelling narrative arc and logical progression.
