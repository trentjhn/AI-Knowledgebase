### The Override Gap: A Magnitude Account of Knowledge Conflict Failure in Hypernetwork-Based Instant LLM Adaptation [2604.23750v1] → context-engineering.md
**Why it matters:** Reveals why 'instant' document internalization often fails to correct an LLM's existing biases and provides a training-free fix. For practitioners, this means we can now boost the reliability of fast-weight-update systems to outperform vanilla RAG on knowledge conflicts by targeting specific 'knowledge-critical' layers.
**Key findings:** The study identifies an 'Override Gap' where hypernetwork-generated LoRA adapters fail to displace 'deeply ingrained' pretrained facts (accuracy drops to 46.4% on deep conflicts). This failure is modeled as a magnitude contest: the document wins only when the adapter margin exceeds the pretrained margin, which scales with training frequency. Selective Layer Boosting (SLB)—multiplying the top 25% of layers by a factor of 1.75—lifts conflict accuracy to 71% while maintaining 97% novel recall.

### Layerwise Convergence Fingerprints for Runtime Misbehavior Detection in Large Language Models [2604.24542v1] → ai-security.md
**Why it matters:** Practitioners can implement LCF as a unified, low-overhead runtime monitor that catches jailbreaks and injections by profiling the model's internal hidden-state transitions. This approach serves as an internal firewall, requiring no auxiliary models or specialized training data to secure LLM deployments.
**Key findings:** Layerwise Convergence Fingerprinting (LCF) identifies misbehavior by monitoring 'representation velocity' (inter-layer hidden state changes), detecting backdoors, jailbreaks, and prompt injections at prefill before token emission. The study reveals a three-band depth stratification: jailbreaks disrupt early layers (L0–L5), prompt injections affect mid layers (L9–L25), and backdoors peak in late layers, allowing a single all-layer statistical test to achieve <1.3% ASR with <0.1% overhead. LCF is tuning-free, requiring only 200 clean calibration samples to establish a Mahalanobis-based threshold for runtime anomaly detection.

### Defusing the Trigger: Plug-and-Play Defense for Backdoored LLMs via Tail-Risk Intrinsic Geometric Smoothing [2604.24162v1] → ai-security.md
**Why it matters:** Provides a plug-and-play defense for practitioners using third-party weights who are concerned about supply chain poisoning. It identifies and neutralizes malicious attention routing at inference time without needing model training or reference datasets.
**Key findings:** Backdoor triggers in LLM weights manifest as 'trigger-induced attention collapse,' where specific heads concentrate heavily on trigger tokens. The TIGS mechanism mitigates this via a three-stage 'detector-executor' loop: tail-risk screening of semantic regions, dual-scale geometric smoothing, and row-level write-back, reducing Attack Success Rates (ASR) from ~98% to <10% with ~13% prefill latency overhead.
